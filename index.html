The Data Science Workflow

To enable data-driven transformation and make Data Science successful as a business case, there are plenty challenges to be mastered. It is optimally performed by a collaborative cross-functional team composed of data analysts, data scientists, data & machine learning engineers and business experts. When bringing in such broad range of professionals who have different resources and skills sets, it is imperative to establish effective communication and a structured workflow in which all parties know what are their contributions and thus enabling everyone to work together and have fun. Here I am sharing an approach that I have been fruitfully using:

WHY
Questions

It starts by identfying a business problem or an urge to optimize a process. Maybe there are projects ideas, or just a bunch of questions to be answered.

​​It needs refinement​ and clear definition of goals. Translating the initial questions into realistic data science questions with impact defined in business KPIs.

HOW
Iterations

​Mapping of different available data sources to understand if ​relevant data is available to address those questions. ​Many times some data is available but the appropriate question is not there. Other times the question is right but the data is not available because it was not framed in the way that will be relevant.

It is an iterative process: going back and forth with business to reformulate the questions and understand what we want to build: if it's something that can be interesting for the business by support it or optimise a process.

WHAT
Machine Learning Product

First, there is the definition of the data pipeline: how to ingest the data while ensuring its quality, perform data cleaning and feature engineering. Next use such data for model development and refinement to find accurate predictions. This is followed by deployment into an interface that gives business access to the model results in a consistent and reliable way.

To have business impact the solutions go beyond developing models in isolation but are rather end-to-end systems that interacts with the people involved in business processes enabling efficient and actionable outcomes.
